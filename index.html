Business Problem
Why this analysis matters
01
Crisis Scale: Ireland's HSE waiting list exceeded 24.6M patient-waits across 2018–2021, with 4.58M patients waiting over 18 months.
02
COVID Impact: Inpatient 18+ month waiters surged +121% in 12 months. The backlog was still accelerating at data cut-off March 2021.
03
Resource Mismatch: 88.2% of all waits are Outpatient, yet planning historically focused on inpatient bed counts.
04
Specialty Inequity: ENT carries a 32% long-wait rate, Eyes 24.6%, Bones 20.1% — delayed care causes permanent harm.
05
Data Gaps: 191 missing HIPE codes, no patient-level IDs, no geographic or outcome linkage.

9 Critical Insights from the HSE Waiting List Analysis

Data-driven findings across volume, trends, specialties, demographics and statistical significance · 2018–2021

Volume & Scale
Insight 01
Outpatient Dominates at 88.2%
Of 24.6M total patient-waits, 21.7M are Outpatient. The inpatient system receives disproportionate policy attention relative to its actual demand share of just 11.8%.
21.7M / 24.6M = 88.2%
Insight 02
1 in 5 Patients Waits Over 18 Months
4.58M patient-waits (18.6%) exceed 18 months — the crisis threshold. This ratio worsened every single year from 2018 onward with no sign of reversal.
4.58M patients · 18.6% rate
Insight 03
2019 Was the Last Year of Improvement
Inpatient volume fell 9.2% from 2018 to 2019 — the only positive trend in the dataset. From 2019 onwards every metric deteriorated, accelerated by COVID-19.
IP 2018→2019: −9.2% ▼
COVID & Statistical Trend
Insight 04
COVID Created a 121% Crisis Surge
Inpatient 18+ month waiters grew from ~5,100 in March 2020 to ~11,300 by March 2021 — a 121% increase in 12 months. The trend was still accelerating at data cut-off.
5,105 → 11,306 in 12 months
Insight 05
Spearman ρ = 0.194 Confirms Deterioration
A Spearman rank correlation of ρ=0.194 (p=0.001) between year and average wait confirms the deterioration is statistically significant and detectable months ahead — enabling proactive intervention.
ρ = 0.194 · p = 0.001
Insight 06
Kruskal-Wallis Confirms Year Effects
H=141.4 (p<0.001) confirms that the distribution of waiting list counts differs significantly across years — COVID is not just a narrative, it is statistically proven in the data.
H = 141.4 · p < 0.001
Specialty & Equity
Insight 07
ENT is the Most Inequitable Specialty
ENT carries the highest 18+ month rate at 32.0% — nearly 1 in 3 patients waiting over 18 months. Eyes (24.6%) and Urine (22.1%) follow. These are not high-volume specialties, meaning the bottleneck is capacity, not demand.
ENT: 32% · Eyes: 24.6%
Insight 08
Children Bear Disproportionate OP Burden
Children (0–15) represent 15.1% of outpatient waits but only 9.6% of inpatient. Extended waits during developmental years carry lifelong consequences that adults do not face.
15.1% OP vs 9.6% IP
Insight 09
65+ Growing Fastest — Pressure Ahead
The 65+ cohort grew +19% over 2018–2020, outpacing all other age groups. As Ireland's population ages, this systemic pressure will compound without structural capacity investment.
65+ cohort: +19% in 2 years
